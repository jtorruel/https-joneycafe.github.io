1,000 Layer Cake

1000-Layer Cake: This Cake is the most traditional in Chile. It has multiple layers of thin crispy dough, almost cookie, and dulce de leche (boiled sweet milk). Our 1000-layer cake contains imported sweet milk from Buenos Aires, Argentina. It also has a buttercream made from scratch, and it's topped with seasonal fruit.

Chilean Donut

Chilean Roscas is an ancient Chilean pastry recipe. Not to be confused with DONUTS because they don't look alike. Chilean donuts are a simple preparation made from wheat flour to which sugar, milk, margarine, egg, and orange zest are added, forming a soft and light dough that is then given its classic shape to fry in oil finally.

Sandwiches




Chacarero (Farmer's steak sandwich)

Chacarero: The chacarero is a typical dish of Chilean gastronomy, consisting of a sandwich made up of chopped green chili peppers, steak slices, cold-cooked green beans, and sliced ​​tomatoes. Mayonnaise is optional, and the original recipe does not include it. Nevertheless, it is a characteristic recipe of the urban food of Santiago.

$12.50
Churrasco Italiano

Churrasco Italiano Churrasco Italiano is a mouth-watering Chilean sandwich made with thin slices of grilled steak, mayonnaise, tomatoes, and palta – a local name for avocados - all tucked into fresh bread rolls. But why is this sandwich dubbed Italiano? It's because each addition represents a color of the Italian flag: avocado for green, mayo for white, and tomatoes for red.

$12.50
Barros Luco

Barros Luco Barros Luco is a simple Chilean sandwich that usually consists of thin slices of grilled beef steak topped with cheese and served inside various buns and bread rolls. It got its unique name after the Chilean president Ramón Barros Luco, who was a great admirer of this satisfying sandwich.

$12.00
